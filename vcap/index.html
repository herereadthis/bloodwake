VCAP Site Request

Narrative

(This section is also on the VCAP site request form)

While the southern half of my property is a lovely, well-draining lawn and garden, the northern half experiences runoff problems after every rain event, which has led to poor vegetation and slight erosion. Runoff from both a neighbor's parcel and my property flow into an impervious driveway, instead of infiltrating the ground. It essentially becomes a muddy river when it rains heavily. That water flows into a storm drain about fifty feet away, which often gets overwhelmed and flooded.

I've tried previous mitigation strategies by creating berms along the border with the neighbor's to promote infiltration. To address my resource concerns, I would like to establish a several rain gardens surrounding my driveway, to absorb the storm water and correct the poor vegetation.

I would also like to add rainwater harvesting to limit the muddy runoff. The collected water would be excellent for the existing and proposed gardens on my parcel. I hope to convert half of my turf backyard and sideyard to conservation landscaping using native plants such as Fothergilla, Eutrochium, and Asclepias, which would create a pollinator habitat.
